### ** Zuber: Ride-Sharing Analysis in Chicago**
As a data analyst for Zuber, a new ride-sharing startup in Chicago, this project focuses on identifying passenger preference patterns and the impact of external factors, such as weather, on trip frequency and duration. The project involved analyzing competitor data and testing statistical hypotheses using both SQL and Python.

**Key Stages & Insights:**
* **Data Extraction (SQL):** Complex queries to group rides by company, filter specific taxi fleets (Yellow/Blue), and classify weather as "Good" or "Bad" using `CASE` and `JOIN` across trip and weather tables.
* **Market Analysis:** Identification of leading taxi companies (Flash Cab and Taxi Affiliation Services) and Chicago's most popular drop-off locations (Top 10 neighborhoods).
* **Data Visualization:** Charts detailing taxi company performance and the distribution of trips across major urban destinations.
* **Hypothesis Testing:** Statistical validation to check if the average duration of trips from the Loop to O'Hare International Airport changes on rainy Saturdays, utilizing Student's t-tests for independent samples.


### **Technical Skills**
* **SQL:** Advanced querying, aggregation, filtering, subqueries, and JOINs across multiple database tables.
* **Python:** Pandas for analysis, Matplotlib/Seaborn for visualization, and Scipy.stats for statistical testing.
* **Statistics:** Hypothesis testing for equality of means, significance level definition (Alpha), and variance analysis.
